About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Harvard University. My research focuses on the decarbonization of energy systems across sectors, particularly the transport and buildings sectors. I believe that interdisciplinary perspectives, combining engineering and social sciences, are crucial to mitigate and adapt to climate change. Two of my key research areas so far include 1) the interaction between the transport and electricity systems, specifically the integration of electric vehicles with emphasis on mobility and charging behavior as well as demand response; 2) the adoption and integration of heat pumps.

Publications

My work has investigated how to support the adoption of low-carbon technologies and how to integrate these technologies into energy systems across sectors, particularly transport and buildings. I focus on sector coupling with heterogeneous behavioral aspects of technology integration, long-term planning at different technology diffusion stages, and both spatial and temporal considerations. A particular focus has been on electrification, particularly electric vehicles, including vehicle-to-grid, and heat pumps. I developed an agent-based model to simulate heterogeneous driving and charging behavior with the goal of leveraging its flexibility for electric vehicle integration into different grid contexts.

As I aim to support the knowledge transfer between academia, industry, policy, and the public, my publications include practitioner articles and blog posts beyond peer-reviewed journal articles.

Supervision and Teaching

One of my passions is to engage with students. I particularly enjoy developing student research projects and discussing them in weekly one-on-one supervision meetings. From undergraduate to graduate courses and from large lectures to small seminars, I find discussions with students inspiring. I have implemented innovative teaching methods, e.g., peer-grading and the Six Sentence Argument (6SA), and developed online teaching videos.
