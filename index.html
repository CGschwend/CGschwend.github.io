Research Profile

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Harvard University, focusing on sustainability in the built environment. My research investigates how to decarbonize energy systems across sectors, particularly the transport and buildings sectors. I believe that interdisciplinary perspectives are crucial to find innovative solutions. Therefore, I combine my background in environmental engineering with data analytics and social sciences. I use a variety of methods, e.g., agent-based modeling, geospatial analyses, choice experiments and interviews, working with large datasets and mostly using Python. I have attracted over 220,000 USD in research grants and fellowships, including from the Swiss National Science Foundation. My many years of experience abroad and growing up in a small village convinced me of the value of diverse perspectives. I am passionate about increasing diversity and inclusion inside and outside of the research community through mentorship, teaching, and outreach.

Publications

My work has investigated how to support the adoption and integration of low-carbon technologies with focus on sector coupling and heterogeneous behavioral aspects, long-term planning at different technology diffusion stages, and both spatial and temporal considerations. One key focus has been electrification, particularly the integration of electric vehicles into different electricity grid contexts with emphasis on (future) mobility and charging behavior as well as controlled charging strategies and vehicle-to-grid.
Ongoing research projects include the combined integration of electric vehicles and heat pumps into the electricity system and possible demand management strategies. I further investigate effective incentives to leverage the flexibility of electricity demand and analyze policy options to accelerate heat pump adoption.

As I aim to support the knowledge transfer between academia, industry, policy, and the public, my publications include practitioner articles and blog posts beyond peer-reviewed journal articles.

Supervision and Teaching

One of my passions is to engage with students from various backgrounds, including engineering, natural sciences, and management studies. I particularly enjoy developing student research projects and discussing them in weekly one-on-one supervision meetings. From undergraduate to graduate courses and from large lectures to small seminars, I find discussions with students inspiring and seek to provide an interdisciplinary perspective on pressing societal challenges, such as the energy transition. I have implemented innovative teaching methods, e.g., peer-grading and the Six Sentence Argument (6SA), and developed online teaching videos.
